The Model

01

Fixed-Term Account Structures

Capital enters our firm through private high-yield loan agreements. Each agreement specifies a principal amount, a term length, and a fixed yield to be returned at maturity. All terms are immutable.

02

Proprietary Cash Management

Lender capital is deployed into proprietary cash management systems developed and operated internally. These systems are designed to generate output in excess of contractual obligations across a defined range of operating conditions.

03

Spread-Based Economics

Revenue is the spread between what the proprietary systems produce and the fixed cost of lender capital. Contractual yields are priced against the modeled worst-case system outcomes.

Lending Structures

Two structures.
One principle.

Bayfall Morrigan provides two distinct fixed-term account structures, each carefully designed to accommodate the varying circumstances and objectives of private lenders. One structure serves those with capital immediately available, offering a direct and fully operational pathway into our firm's funding framework. The other supports those who prefer to build their allocation progressively, advancing over time through a structured, guided process. Both pathways are constructed to provide transparency, consistency, and alignment with our operational principles.

I
Standard Structure

Standard Private
High-Yield Loan

How it works

Lenders commit a predetermined lump sum through a private high-yield loan agreement. A 6.5% origination fee is taken at inception, after which the selected term begins. Three term lengths are available: 3, 6, or 12 months.

Each term carries a different fixed monthly yield that compounds every 28 days. Longer terms carry higher rates and produce significantly greater total returns. The stated yield is locked at execution.

At maturity

The lender has preselected either a lump sum payout of the full balance, or scheduled monthly distributions over an agreed period, with the same yield continuing to accrue on any remaining balance throughout.

II
Alternative Structure

Account
Build-Up Program

Accumulation phase

For lenders whose intended allocation exceeds what is currently available. A target balance is established and funded through weekly contributions over a defined period. The weekly contribution size determines the yield rate, larger contributions produce higher rates.

Transition to loan

Yield compounds identically to the standard structure and is added to the balance, total capital contributed is typically less than the target balance itself. No origination fee applies. Once the target is reached, the account transitions automatically into a pre-agreed standard high-yield loan on terms set before accumulation begins.

Our Capital Model

Why we use private lenders.

This phase is building something larger than itself.

A track record is a structural asset.

Building proprietary systems that generate consistent returns is one thing. Building a firm with a verifiable history of accepting fixed obligations and honoring every one of them on schedule is another. The private lending phase of our operation is where that record is established. Every contract executed and settled at the promised yield advances a body of evidence that no marketing material can substitute for.

Institutional relationships require institutional credibility.

The capital structures required to work with institutional counterparties are not accessible to firms without a demonstrated history of obligation fulfillment at scale. Private lenders give us the environment to build that history deliberately, at a pace that keeps our obligations well within what our systems are built to support. The goal is not to stay here. The goal is to build through here.

Larger capital requires purpose-built systems.

Institutional-scale capital does not simply run through the same systems at a larger size. It requires systems designed from the ground up to operate at that range efficiently. The retained earnings generated through the private lending phase fund the development of those next-generation systems in parallel, so that when the track record is in place, the infrastructure already is too.

Legal Framework

Fixed obligations.
Honored without exception

Capital received through our fixed-term account structures constitutes a private loan, not an investment. Each agreement specifies a fixed principal, a fixed yield, and a fixed maturity. The return is contractually owed regardless of firm performance, which means the instruments do not satisfy the conditions courts use to classify something as a security under federal law. There is no shared enterprise, no variable return tied to our efforts, and no participation in firm upside. Michigan's lending licensing requirements attach to the party extending credit, not to the borrower. We receive funds. We do not lend them to others. No lending license applies. Every lender receives complete written disclosure of these characterizations before committing capital.
