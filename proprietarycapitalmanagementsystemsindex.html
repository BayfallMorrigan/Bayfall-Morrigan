The Systems

Built on understanding.
Validated by data.

Bayfall Morrigan develops quantitative trading algorithms designed to operate exclusively in equities markets using high-frequency strategies. The defining characteristic of how these systems are built is not the technology behind them. It is the starting point. Where most quantitative systems are constructed by extracting patterns from large historical datasets, ours begin with something that cannot be found in data alone: a structural understanding of price action and market psychology. We identify a specific behavior that exists in the market, define precisely why it exists and under what conditions it appears, and then build a data collection and validation process around that thesis. The data does not generate the structure. The structure determines the data.

What that produces is a system whose merit does not depend on what the broader market is doing. Because our algorithms are built on the microscopic, fundamental mechanics of how equities move, not on directional trends or macroeconomic conditions, they carry their edge regardless of whether markets are rising, falling, or ranging. No broad market movement is large enough to sweep out a position we were never holding.

How They're Built

A four-stage development architecture.

Every system moves through the same four-stage architecture, in the same sequence, without exception. The process does not end at deployment. It runs continuously as long as the system operates.

01
Thesis Construction

Every system begins with a precisely defined structural thesis. Before a single data point is collected, we define what market behavior we are looking for, why it exists, and what it looks like when it appears.

02
Historical & Live Sample Collection

Data collection begins with historical performance and carries forward into continuous live sampling. Live data is collected at a microscopic level, giving us a real-time picture of how our systems are behaving and how that behavior is shifting over time. That same dataset lets us model the effect of structural adjustments before committing to them.

03
Proprietary Simulation

That data feeds a simulation engine that stress-tests the system across tens of thousands of independent scenarios. Slippage, sizing constraints, and friction are all modeled explicitly. Output is a full behavioral profile from best case to worst.

04
Configuration Optimization

The simulation outputs are evaluated across multiple diverse operating profiles. Each profile is tested to identify what varibles in the configuration produce the most favorable results with the most controlled risk. The output is not a single answer. It is a defined operating specification that tells us exactly how the system should be deployed.

Continuous loop — Forward looking live sampling
Design Philosophy

Built on what the market
never stops doing.

Our systems are not designed around trends, momentum, or macroeconomic conditions. They are built on the microscopic psychological patterns that drive how equities move at their most fundamental level, the behaviors that exist because human decision-making exists. Those patterns do not disappear in a bull market or a bear market. They do not change when interest rates move or when sentiment shifts. They are structural constants, present in the market regardless of what surrounds them.

That is the foundation our algorithms are built on and because the edge is derived from something that fundamental, it is not fragile. Broader market conditions do not erode it. Regimes change. The underlying psychology does not.

Risk & Exposure

Our edge lives in short windows. By design.

Capital is deployed only when a precisely defined structural condition is confirmed. When that condition is not present, the system is not active.

There is no passive exposure, no held positions, no sensitivity to what the broader market does overnight or over a quarter. The system enters, executes, and returns to neutral. This is deliberate. By operating on the microscopic mechanics of how equities move rather than on broader market direction, our systems carry no meaningful exposure to the kind of sweeping market events that damage conventional strategies. A broad selloff, a macro shift, an unexpected regime change. None of these are large enough to affect a position we were never holding. The result is a risk profile that is defined by the precision of the entry condition, not by the temperament of the market.

Scale & Precision

Smaller is more efficient.
That is the design.

Large institutions operate at a scale that makes precision-dependent systems structurally impractical. When capital deployment scales, the mechanics that give our systems their edge stop functioning efficiently.

It is not that our approach is unknown. It is that the firms managing capital at institutional scale cannot operate within the constraints our approach requires. Our systems are built for a capital range where that precision is still actionable. That is the range we stay in. Lower-denomination capital distributed across a wider base of lenders is not a constraint we are managing around. It is the configuration the systems were built for, and it is where they perform most efficiently and most reliably.
